Most places carry a disaster history that no one has ever really seen.

Weather apps tell you about tomorrow. Almost nothing tells you what a place has already lived through. Vestige is the memory layer — every recorded extreme event, where it struck, what it cost, and the public sources that prove it.

Not forecasts. Not opinions. A record. The kind of thing you want before you buy a house, write a story, plan a season, or simply understand the ground under your feet.

Why it's free

Public record, public map.

Every event here comes from data your taxes already paid for. The map stays free — no ads, no paywall on the past. Deeper tools for journalists, planners, and researchers can come later, and keep it running. The public memory stays public.
