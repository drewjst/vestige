What the map is built from.

Vestige is a read-only atlas over three authoritative feeds. Every event links back to its source row — nothing is modeled, summed, or invented here.

USGS Earthquake Hazards

Global · recorded since 1900 · live catalog

ComCat / FDSNWS GeoJSON — precise epicenter points with magnitude and depth. The atlas renders each quake as a sized point.

USGS records where and how strong, never ground impact. Vestige shows “impact not recorded by this source” — not a zero.

IBTrACS / NHC

Global best track · recorded since 1842

International Best Track Archive for Climate Stewardship — position and intensity every few hours, drawn as cyclone tracks on the map.

Track data is physical observation of the storm center, not landfall impact. Deaths and damage for U.S. landfalls, when they exist, come from Storm Events — not IBTrACS.

NOAA NCEI Storm Events

United States · recorded since 1996

The only feed that carries recorded deaths, injuries, and property damage — tornadoes, floods, heat, winter storms, wildfires, wind, and more.

Collection is heterogeneous: some rows arrive with observed coordinates (county-collected tornadoes, flash floods); others are logged at the NWS forecast-zone level and attributed to a county honestly — a zone name is never drawn as a pinpoint.

Reference layers (not event feeds)

County polygons come from U.S. Census TIGER/Line. NWS forecast-zone → county attribution uses NOAA’s public zone–county correlation — administrative attachment, not measured fire or flood extent.

  • No place-level totals — impacts stay itemized; Vestige never sums deaths or damage across events.
  • Precision is visible — measured points, track lines, and county attribution render differently on the map.
  • Absence is data — a zero in Storm Events is shown as recorded; silence from USGS/IBTrACS is labeled, not filled in.
