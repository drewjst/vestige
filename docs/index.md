# Vestige

**A historical atlas of extreme natural-hazard events — every place keeps a record.**

Vestige is a map-first record of extreme natural hazards — earthquakes, tropical cyclones, floods,
tornadoes, heat and winter storms. Open the map, click a place, and see what actually happened
there, with a trail back to the source for every event.

It is **history, not forecast** — what was *recorded*, not what a model *predicts*. And it runs on
**free, public data** (USGS, NOAA/NCEI, IBTrACS), kept free to look at.

## What makes it different
- **Recorded, not modeled.** Every event is a real record from an authoritative source, linked back
  to it. No synthetic estimates dressed up as fact.
- **Honest about what it knows.** Geometry precision, whether impact was recorded, and whether a
  summary was generated are shown as first-class facts. See [The honesty model](honesty-model.md).
- **One map, many hazards.** Geophysical and meteorological events in a single place-keyed atlas.
